# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
## Cybercrime Awareness – Task 5, Part A

---

## What Is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in
India. It involves criminals tricking individuals into authorising fraudulent transactions
or sharing sensitive credentials through social engineering techniques.

---

## A Realistic Scenario

Riya, a 19-year-old college student in Bhopal, listed her old laptop for sale on a popular
classifieds website. Within hours, she received a message from "Vikram," who claimed
to be an Army officer interested in buying it.

**Step 1 – Building Trust:** Vikram spoke politely and agreed to pay the full asking price
immediately, without bargaining – a classic red flag.

**Step 2 – The Hook:** He said he would pay via UPI and sent Riya a QR code, asking
her to "scan and accept" the money.

**Step 3 – The Trick:** The QR code was actually a payment *request*, not a transfer.
By scanning and entering her UPI PIN, Riya *sent* money instead of receiving it.

**Step 4 – Escalation:** Vikram called again claiming the first transfer "failed" and
asked her to try once more. Riya lost Rs. 18,000 across two transactions before
realising what had happened.

---

## Who Is Targeted?

First-time sellers, students, elderly users, and anyone unfamiliar with the difference
between UPI payment requests and payment transfers.

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss (often unrecoverable)
- Psychological distress and loss of trust in digital platforms
- Loss of personal data if the fraudster captured screen interactions

---

*Source: This is a realistic fictional scenario based on common UPI fraud patterns
documented by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) and RBI advisories.*
