# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
## For College Students in India

---

### General Online Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a passphrase like
   "Mango$Rain#2024" – long and memorable. Never reuse passwords across platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts including
   email, social media, and banking apps.

3. **Never click on suspicious links** in SMS, WhatsApp, or email – even if they appear
   to come from known senders. Always verify through the official website.

4. **Keep your apps and phone updated** – security patches fix known vulnerabilities
   that scammers exploit.

5. **Do not share OTPs, PINs, or passwords** with anyone over call, chat, or email –
   no legitimate bank, app, or government body will ever ask for these.

6. **Use trusted Wi-Fi only** – avoid logging into banking or academic portals on
   public Wi-Fi at cafes, bus stations, or college canteens without a VPN.

---

### Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

7. **Understand the difference between QR codes** – scanning a QR code to *receive*
   money is always wrong. You only scan to *pay*. If someone sends you a QR code
   to "receive" money, it is a scam. Report and block immediately.

8. **Never enter your UPI PIN to accept money** – your UPI PIN is only needed when
   you are sending money. No legitimate payer will ask for your PIN.

9. **Verify the recipient's UPI ID carefully** before every transfer – one wrong
   character sends money to a stranger with no way to recover it.

10. **Set daily UPI transaction limits** in your banking app to minimise loss in case
    of unauthorised access.

---

### If You Are a Victim

- Report immediately at: **cybercrime.gov.in**
- Call the **National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930** (available 24×7)
- Also report to your bank's fraud helpline to attempt a transaction reversal
- File a complaint at your nearest police station under the IT Act
