# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
## A Responsible Digital Presence Checklist

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profile professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a
   bio that reflects your academic/professional interests.

2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor,
   future employer, or family saw this?" If not, do not post it.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and
   personal information on each platform.

4. **Give credit** – Always credit the original creator when sharing someone else's
   content, images, or ideas.

5. **Engage positively** – Use social media to learn, connect, and contribute to
   meaningful discussions in your field of study.

6. **Separate personal and professional profiles** – Consider keeping a LinkedIn for
   professional networking and limiting personal sharing to trusted circles.

7. **Report harmful content** – If you see cyberbullying, misinformation, or harmful
   content, use the platform's reporting tools.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Don't share personal details publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home
   address, college timetable, or financial information on public platforms.

2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** – Do not participate in online
   harassment, and do not stay silent if a peer is being targeted.

3. **Don't spread unverified information** – Always check a source before sharing
   news, statistics, or claims. Misinformation spreads fast and causes real harm.

4. **Don't post in anger or frustration** – Heated posts about classmates, faculty, or
   institutions can have lasting professional consequences and cannot be fully deleted.

5. **Don't accept unknown friend/follow requests** – Fake accounts are common tools
   for scams, phishing, and social engineering attacks.

6. **Don't use social media during exams or important deadlines** – It damages focus
   and academic performance.

7. **Don't overshare your location** – Avoid tagging your precise location in real-time,
   especially when you are alone or in an unfamiliar place.
